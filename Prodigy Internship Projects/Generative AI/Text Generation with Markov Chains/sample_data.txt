The sky above the port was the color of television, tuned to a dead channel. It was not like it wasn't trying, but the rain had been falling for days, washing away the neon grime of the city. He walked down the street, his coat pulled tight against the chill. The street was empty, save for a stray cat that scurried into an alleyway.

"Where are you going?" asked the voice in his head.
"Nowhere," he muttered aloud, startling himself.
The cat looked back, eyes glowing green in the darkness. It seemed to know something he didn't. Maybe it knew the way out.

Far away, a siren wailed. It was a lonely sound, echoing through the canyons of steel and glass. He quickened his pace. There was a meeting tonight, and he couldn't be late. Not again. The others would be waiting. The ones with the answers. Or so they claimed.

He turned the corner and saw the sign. "The Rusty Spoon." It was a dive bar, the kind where dreams went to die and were reborn as regrets. Perfect. He pushed open the door and stepped into the smoke-filled room.
